O2 Rhythm Regulation

Overwhelmed Reset

A short reset for moments when your nervous system feels overloaded, flooded, overstimulated, or mentally stuck.

2–4 Minutes Low Stimulation Beginner Friendly

When to use this

Use this reset when your thoughts are racing, your chest feels tight, your body feels tense, or you feel mentally overloaded from stress, work, noise, conflict, or emotional buildup.

Reset Steps

1

Slow your environment down

Pause for a moment. Sit down if possible. Reduce extra stimulation around you. Lower volume, stop scrolling, or step away from unnecessary input.

2

Exhale longer than you inhale

Breathe in slowly through your nose for 4 seconds. Exhale slowly for 6 seconds. Repeat several rounds without forcing deep breaths.

3

Reconnect to physical sensation

Press your feet into the floor. Relax your jaw. Notice the chair beneath you or the feeling of your hands touching together.

4

Reduce mental pressure

You do not need to solve everything right now. Focus only on the next small action in front of you.

Safety Note

This regulation tool is intended for stress reduction and nervous system support. It is not medical or mental health treatment and should not replace professional care when needed.

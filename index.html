FAQ

Straight answers for chiefs, supervisors, training staff, and wellness teams.

Is this therapy or treatment?

No. O₂ Rhythm is not therapy and is not a treatment for mental health conditions. It is a set of short, skills‑based breathing and regulation drills designed to support operational readiness and recovery between demands. It can sit alongside peer support, EAP, and clinical resources your agency already uses.

How long does a reset take?

Most protocols run between 30 and 120 seconds. They are built to fit into the gaps that actually exist on shift: between calls, after a bad one, before briefing, or while you are staged and waiting.

Do people need an app or account?

No. Operators scan a QR with the camera they already use. There are no logins, no downloads, and no personal accounts for them to manage.

What data do you collect?

Usage is tracked at the environment/menu level so you can see what is being used and where. Personal identifying information is not required for operators to access protocols.

Who usually sponsors a pilot?

Most pilots are sponsored by a mix of operations, training, wellness/peer support, and leadership who want a tool that fits into real work instead of adding another program to the calendar.

Can we see example placements and menus?

Yes. During the pilot intake process you can request sample placement plans and environment menus for dispatch, patrol, fire/EMS, corrections, and training. Those examples help tailor QR locations and language to your agency.

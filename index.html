FAQ

Is this just breathing exercises?

No. Some protocols use breath, but the core of O₂ Rhythm is short, environment‑specific drills designed for consoles, bays, units, and control rooms. Crews are taught to regulate in the same conditions they work in, not on a mat in a quiet room.

How long does a protocol take?

Most protocols run 30–120 seconds. The goal is a measurable down‑shift that fits between calls, after a critical event, or before returning to the floor-not a 20‑minute session that pulls people off the line.

Do crews need an app or login?

No. Crews scan a QR code or tap a link posted in their environment. There are no usernames, passwords, or downloads to manage.

What does “adoption tracked by environment” mean?

Each placement has its own QR, so usage is tied to that console, bay, unit, or room. You can see where protocols are actually being used and adjust placements or menus accordingly.

How do we start a pilot?

Open the demo, then use the pilot form below to share your environments and objectives. We’ll propose a 60–90 day pilot outline, including placement plan, recommended protocols, and simple adoption metrics.

Can we partner or bring this into our organization’s programs?

Yes. We partner with agencies, associations, and training organizations to embed O₂ Rhythm into existing wellness, resilience, peer support, and academy curricula. Use the pilot form to flag “partnership” and we’ll set up a call.

What does success look like in a pilot?

We focus on three things: placements that actually get used, repeat usage by crews, and clear feedback from line staff and leaders about where protocols help the most. You get simple environment‑level usage data, not a new dashboard to live in.

Can this support peer support or chaplain teams?

Yes. Many agencies use O₂ Rhythm as a tool that peer support, chaplains, and wellness leads can bring into conversations, briefings, and post‑incident check‑ins, so regulation is built into the culture, not just a one‑off training.

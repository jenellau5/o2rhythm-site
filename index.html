Where it gets used

O₂ Rhythm isn’t generic “breathing.” It’s a reset system placed inside the environments where stress spikes. Each environment gets a menu that matches the moment.

Dispatch + Console Environments

For after a high-acuity call, after a difficult caller, or before you pick up the next line. Designed for fast downshift without losing operational readiness.

Console Reset
Short resets that reduce lingering activation and bring attention back online.
Placement: console edge • monitor stand • headset station
Between-Call Reset

Placement: phone stand • CAD station • supervisor desk
Quiet Room Reset

FAQ

Clear answers. No fluff.

How is pricing determined?

Pricing is based on scope: number of environments (console/bay/unit/etc.), number of QR placements, and your update cadence tier (monthly vs quarterly). Larger deployments and custom requests change scope.

Is it customizable for my agency/team?

Yes. Menus are built around your environments and operational reality. Dispatch differs from patrol. EMS differs from corrections. Training differs from post-incident. The system stays consistent; the menus are tailored.

Do people need an app or login?

No. QR → page opens. That’s the point.

How do you keep it from becoming ignored wall clutter?

Placement + relevance + champions + measurable usage. If adoption is low, you’ll see it quickly and adjust what’s needed.

How often do you update content?

Updates are provided monthly or quarterly depending on tier. If something operational changes (policy, environment, critical incident need), additional updates can be made when needed.
